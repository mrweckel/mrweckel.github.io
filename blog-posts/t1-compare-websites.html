Comparing My Favorite Websites

August 08, 2014

In all the time I spend online reading the standard mixture of enlightening articles, current events, and just plain crap, until now I never really questioned what attracted me to these sites. I guess I always assumed it was the content. Good or bad. The content is what drove me there. But is it what kept me there?

Three of my favorite sites. Boom. Here you go: IFLScience, Gothamist and The Verge. Check em just about everyday. Sometimes I feel great when Im done, and sometimes I just cant get over how much time I regularly give away as if it were in never ending supply. I love these sites because I can quickly jump in to their content, learn a thing or two, and then jump back out quickly and easily.

As soon as you visit IFLScience, you are hit in the face with mind-bending imagery and some really loud colors. Not for everyone. But for me, when it comes to my science, I want it sexy and high-def, and slightly dumbed down. I can easily scroll thorough articles for a long while, and although there is strong clutter of animated gif ads and other assaults on the senses, most of the time I hardly notice it.

The Verge is another site that greats you with in-your-face colors, large images, and host full of ads. On top of all that it throws on top a rainbow-style hero module that I think contains contain. But who the hell can tell through all the crazy colors. I check The Verge mainly for the tech articles, but I suppose I could get the same news from Mashable, Wired, reddit/r/technology, and any of the other hundreds of tech blogs.

Now The Gothamist is my guilty rag sheet pleasure. It is not as colorful and sensory as the two previous sites I have discussed, but the content (while under the guise of NYC news), is just as big of an assault on the senses. Itâ€™s layout is simple, especially on a smart phone. I scroll down, read a bunch of news I never really needed to know, and I move on. Sometimes simple is exactly what my brain needs.

Well, this exercise has reminded just how much of my life I spend, and will continue to spend, staring at a screen of some kind or another. It has also reminded me of how much I hate blogging, especially when maybe 2 people will read this. If you have made it this far, please get up from the computer and go for a walk. Cheers.
